Why Epistemic Exists

The sentence I kept losing.

I used to confuse listening with learning. I'd hit the gym or commute with Modern Wisdom on, catch a sharp idea, and feel smart and productive instantly, like I owned it.

By the next morning, 90% of it was gone. "Poof, see ya later..."

I hated being mid-conversation, going — 'it's like… there's actually a word for this, I heard it last week, basically… ugh, nevermind.'

So, I tried to fix it:

  • ➣ Readwise highlights — never reopened.
  • ➣ Podcast summaries — bullet points I'd forget.
  • ➣ Notion dashboards — my little cheat sheet.

None of it stuck. I just had a better-organised version of the same problem.

Passive listening is just mental masturbation... and a broken learning format.

But along the way, I realised other people — expats, non-native English speakers, podcast lovers — were stumbling on their words too.

So I built the tool I wished existed.

Epistemic isn't trying to make you smarter. It's just trying to make sure what you already know doesn't die before it matters.

If you've ever gone 'nevermind' mid-sentence — this is for you.
